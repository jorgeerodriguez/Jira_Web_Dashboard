Initiative & Feature Target Dates

Monte Carlo delivery forecast for open Initiatives and Features. Each item's remaining child scope is burned down by simulating its recent weekly completion rate 10,000 times; the dates below are the P50 (coin-flip) and P85 (safer commit) finish dates. As of July 1, 2026.

Initiative

Rolled-up scope and throughput, with its Features nested beneath.

Features — forecastable

Standalone Features (not under an Initiative above), sorted by P85 (safer) target date. Pace λ = child items completed per week (recent 12 weeks). Confidence reflects how much recent data backs the rate.

Features — no forecast yet

Standalone Features that are complete, stalled (no completions in 12 weeks), or not broken into stories.

Method. For each item, remaining scope R = child items not Done (excluding "Will Not Do"). Pace λ = children completed in the last 12 weeks ÷ min(12, weeks-since-created, floored at 2). Each of 10,000 trials draws weekly completions ~ Poisson(λ) until R is burned down; P50/P85/P95 are percentiles of the resulting week counts, added to the as-of date. Simulation is seeded, so results are stable across reloads.
Completion = child entered the Done status (from the changelog; resolutiondate is unreliable here). Child→parent via the parent field.
Known limitations (prototype). (1) Scope growth not modeled — Features usually add stories as they progress, so dates skew optimistic; the Initiative is especially a floor because 3 of its 5 Features have no stories yet. (2) Uses item-level pace, which assumes current staffing holds. (3) Blocked items are still counted in scope but can't complete until unblocked — treat blocked-heavy items with caution. (4) Count-based — a large story and a tiny one weigh the same.
Next steps if this proves useful: model scope-growth from history, use per-assignee throughput, and add a cycle-time/aging view to spot at-risk in-progress items.

Lead Time & Cycle Time

For delivered DEVOPS stories under the open Initiative & Features. Lead time = created → Done (total elapsed, including backlog wait). Cycle time = time actively in progress (In Progress / Blocked / On Hold / Validating), summed across spells; direct To-Do→Done counts as 1 day. The gap between them is queue/wait time. Completion measured from status history; "Will Not Do" excluded. As of July 1, 2026.

Lead-time distribution

How long delivered stories took end-to-end. Most work should cluster left; a long right tail signals stories that sat in the backlog or dragged.

By Feature

Median lead vs. median cycle per Feature (delivered stories). A big lead–cycle gap = lots of waiting; similar values = work flows straight through.

Definitions. Lead time = calendar days from created to the Done transition. Cycle time = summed time in active statuses (In Progress, Blocked, On Hold, Validating, Reviewing); reopened stories sum every active spell; a story that went straight To-Do→Done with no active spell is recorded as 1 day. Queue = lead − cycle.
Data. Status history pulled per-issue from the Jira changelog for the 86 done stories under the currently-open tracked Features; completion date is the Done-category transition (resolutiondate is unreliable in this instance). Refreshed as of July 1, 2026.
Caveat. Counts stories, not effort — a one-line change and a multi-day build both count as one. Flow efficiency = cycle ÷ lead (how much of the elapsed time was active work).
